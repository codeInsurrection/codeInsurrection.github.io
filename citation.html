Diabetic kidney disease is one of the most serious long-term complications of diabetes and remains the leading cause of kidney failure worldwide1,2. Approximately 20–40% of patients with diabetes develop chronic kidney disease3. As a result, diabetic kidney disease accounts for nearly two out of every five patients requiring dialysis4.

This clinical trial is the first to evaluate an EHR-integrated, alert-based CDS tool designed to increase guideline-recommended UACR screening among adults with type 2 diabetes. The CDS alert was associated with more than a two-fold increase in UACR ordering and more frequent detection of albuminuria. Although an annual UACR assessment for patients with type 2 diabetes has been recommended by professional society guidelines5,6, large clinical investigations have consistently shown that only up to 50% of adults with type 2 diabetes undergo annual albuminuria screening8,9,22. In the control arm of our trial, only 11.5% of patients received UACR screening orders within 90 days, indicating persistent underutilization of UACR testing in routine clinical practice. Contributing factors may include reliance on eGFR alone, competing demands during brief ambulatory visits, and limited awareness of both overdue laboratory studies and guideline recommendations supporting annual UACR19, 4-7 testing in individuals with type 2 diabetes.
