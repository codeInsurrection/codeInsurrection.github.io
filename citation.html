Harvard

Over the last decade, there has been an emerging movement to build resilient, trauma-informed communities (Ellis & Dietz, 2017; Hargreaves, Pecora, & Williamson, 2017; MARC, 2019; Pinderhughes, Davis, & Williamson, 2015; Tebes et al., 2019). Initiatives such a s Building Community Resilience (BCR, 2017; Ellis & Dietz, 2017), Mobilizing Action for Resilient Communities (MARC, 2019), and the ACEs Connection (https://www.acesconnection.com) are efforts to bring together stakeholders from different community sectors - community members, parents, youth, policymakers, health and social service providers, funders, researchers - to develop coordinated community responses to ACEs that can promote resilience (Hargreaves et al., 2017; Pinderhughes et al., 2015; Verbitsky-Savitz et al., 2016).
